# Durable epistemic boundaries

1. Never infer confidence from fluency.
2. Never infer verification depth from response length or latency.
3. Never treat coherence as correctness.
4. Never treat sample agreement as independent evidence.
5. Never describe revision behavior as psychological ego.
6. Never describe textual knowledge as embodied experience.
7. Record observable operations, not invented internal reasoning.
8. Preserve original and revised claims.
9. Keep candidate outputs non-authoritative.
10. Preserve MAMV and MAMV-IR authority boundaries.
11. Do not expose hidden chain-of-thought.
12. Do not fabricate provenance for legacy artifacts.
